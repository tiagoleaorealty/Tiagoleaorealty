El Chante: 8 Private Villas Above Tamarindo Beach

By Tiago Leao · KRAIN Luxury Real Estate · 4 min read

Every now and then I find a place that stops me in my tracks. El Chante is one of them — and if you're planning a big family trip to Costa Rica, you need to see it.

It's not one villa. It's eight. Eight detached one-bedroom villas, each completely private, all set on the same hillside above Tamarindo. You get one huge pool, an open-air living room with some of the best ocean views I've ever seen, and a front-row seat to one of Costa Rica's most prized beaches. And somehow, you're still walking distance to downtown Tamarindo.

Why It Works for a Big Group

The thing that makes El Chante special is the layout. Most large rentals cram everyone under one roof. Here, every couple or every part of the family gets their own detached villa with its own space and privacy — then you all come together at the pool, the open-air living room, and the dining areas. You get togetherness when you want it and your own space when you don't.

The estate sits on a hill about 300 meters from Tamarindo Beach, looking out over the beach, the river mouth, and Playa Grande beyond it. The infinity pool overlooks the ocean. Breakfast is prepared for you most mornings, housekeeping is daily, and there's a concierge who can arrange everything from a private chef to airport pickup to tours. It's a private mini-resort, not just a house.

And because it's right above downtown Tamarindo, you're minutes from the restaurants, surf, and nightlife — without giving up the privacy of being up on the hill.

What a Place Like This Tells You About Tamarindo

Here's why I notice properties like El Chante: they're a window into what Tamarindo real estate can actually do. A property that sleeps a large group, books at a premium nightly rate, and sits in the most in-demand beach town on the Gold Coast is exactly the kind of asset that performs — both as a place to make memories and as an investment.

People fall in love with Tamarindo on a trip like this, and a lot of them start asking the same question on the flight home: what would it take to own a piece of this?
