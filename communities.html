Guanacaste's Gold Coast

The Communities

Ten beach towns, ten completely different lives. From the walkable energy of Tamarindo to the wellness quiet of Nosara, here is what it's actually like to live, buy, and own in each — from an agent who works this coast every day. Current listings included where I have them.

Established Beach Town · ≈30 min from Liberia Airport

Playas del Coco

Coco is the most established beach town in Guanacaste and the closest to Liberia International Airport — about half an hour door to door, which matters more than people expect when they start flying in and out regularly. It's a real working town with supermarkets, banks, clinics, and a long-standing expat community, wrapped around a lively main street and a wide, calm bay known for sport fishing and diving.

For buyers, Coco is the value-and-convenience play: the condo market here is among the most affordable on the coast, rental demand is steady year-round thanks to accessibility, and neighboring Ocotal and Playa Hermosa add quieter, more residential options minutes away. If you want beach life with city practicality and the shortest airport run in Guanacaste, start here.

Calm Bay · ≈1 hr from Liberia Airport

Potrero

Potrero sits one bay north of Flamingo, and that proximity is exactly the opportunity: you're five minutes from the marina, the restaurants, and the white sand — at meaningfully lower prices. The bay itself is calm and swimmable, the pace is local and unhurried, and the growing community of American and Canadian families here tends to be people who did their homework.

The Surfside section, closest to the beach, is a grid of homes and condos that rents well and keeps appreciating as Flamingo's growth spills north. A lot of the smart money on this coast looks at Potrero first: it's the classic "next-door discount" that closes a little more every year the marina develops.

Upscale Marina Town · ≈1 hr from Liberia Airport

Playa Flamingo

Flamingo is the polished end of the Gold Coast: a genuinely white-sand beach, hillsides stacked with ocean-view estates, and a modern marina that changed the town's trajectory — sport fishing, yachting, and the restaurants and services that follow them. It draws buyers who want luxury and calm rather than nightlife, and it delivers both.

The real estate here skews premium: hillside homes with sunset views over the Catalinas Islands, marina-adjacent condos, and a steady pipeline of high-end new construction. Flamingo properties hold value exceptionally well because supply on those hills is finite and the marina keeps raising the floor. If Tamarindo is the Gold Coast's energy, Flamingo is its address.

Resort Community · ≈1 hr from Liberia Airport

Playa Conchal & Brasilito

Conchal's beach is made of crushed shells — turquoise water over white shell sand, regularly ranked among Costa Rica's most beautiful. Behind it sits Reserva Conchal, the Gold Coast's flagship gated resort community: a Robert Trent Jones II golf course, a private beach club, the Westin and W hotels, and a portfolio of condos and homes with full resort amenities and security.

Conchal is the turnkey choice: buyers who want luxury handled — landscaping, rentals, maintenance, golf — rather than managed themselves. Next door, the beach town of Brasilito offers the opposite flavor: an authentic, sandy-street pueblo where value still exists walking distance from a world-class beach. The two together cover both ends of the spectrum.

Protected Surf Town · ≈1 hr 10 min from Liberia Airport

Playa Grande

Playa Grande sits directly across the estuary from Tamarindo — two minutes by boat — and could not be more different. The beach is inside Las Baulas National Park, one of the world's most important leatherback turtle nesting sites, which caps development permanently: low density, no high-rises, dark nights, and one of the most consistent surf breaks in Costa Rica largely to yourself.

That protection is the investment story. Supply here can never meaningfully grow, while demand from surfers and privacy-seekers keeps rising. Homes sit on quiet jungle streets a short walk from the sand, and Tamarindo's restaurants and services are a boat ride away when you want them. For buyers who want nature-forward quiet with town access, Grande is the sleeper pick.

The Hub · ≈1 hr 15 min from Liberia Airport

Tamarindo

Tamarindo is the Gold Coast's engine: the most developed, most walkable, most alive beach town in Guanacaste. Restaurants, surf schools, international schools, medical clinics, nightlife, and a year-round calendar of tourists who become renters and renters who become buyers. If you want to step out your door to coffee, surf, and dinner without a car, this is the one.

It's also the most liquid real estate market on the coast — properties here simply sell faster, because the buyer pool never dries up. Condos start around $200K and ocean-view homes climb past $2 million. Short-term rental demand is the strongest in the region. Quiet Oceanfront · ≈1 hr 20 min from Liberia Airport

Playa Langosta

Langosta is Tamarindo's quiet side — a residential peninsula ten minutes' walk south of town, where the party noise disappears and the streets turn leafy. Oceanfront condos and private homes face a rockier, more dramatic stretch of coast with a famous river-mouth surf break, and sunsets that are somehow better than next door.

This is the "best of both" pick: full walking access to Tamarindo's restaurants and services, none of its late-night volume. Langosta property carries a premium over central Tamarindo for exactly that reason, and its oceanfront condos are some of the most reliable rental performers on the coast — couples and families who want quiet consistently book them.

Surf & Golf Estates · ≈1 hr 30 min from Liberia Airport

Avellanas & Hacienda Pinilla

Playa Avellanas is one of Costa Rica's legendary surf beaches — home of the "Little Hawaii" break — with a barefoot, boards-on-the-truck character that has resisted overdevelopment. Right beside it stretches Hacienda Pinilla: 4,500 gated acres of luxury homes, an 18-hole golf course, a private beach club, and the JW Marriott, all connected by quiet internal roads through dry tropical forest.

The combination is unique on the coast: raw surf-town soul and manicured estate living, ten minutes apart and fifteen from Tamarindo. Pinilla has become one of the strongest luxury markets in Guanacaste — large modern villas with golf and ocean views that rent exceptionally well to high-end groups. It's where several of my current listings sit, and where I spend a lot of my weeks.

Emerging Surf Coast · ≈1 hr 50 min from Liberia Airport

Marbella

Marbella is the stretch most people drive past — and that's precisely its appeal. Between Avellanas and Nosara, this piece of coast has powerful, uncrowded surf, long empty beaches, and a tiny community of people who found it early. Infrastructure is simpler out here, the roads are dustier, and the ocean views cost a fraction of what they do an hour in either direction.

This is the frontier play on the Gold Coast: buy land or a home now, while the paving crews and mini-supers slowly work their way down the coast, and let the Nosara effect creep north. It's not for everyone — you trade convenience for space and price — but buyers who wanted "Nosara fifteen years ago" are looking at exactly this coastline.

Wellness Capital · ≈2 hrs+ from Liberia Airport

Nosara

Nosara is intentional in a way no other Costa Rican beach town is. The community has fought for decades to keep it low-key — no big chains, strict development limits, jungle buffers protecting the beach — and the result is world-famous: the surf at Playa Guiones, a deep yoga and wellness culture, top international schools, and residents who chose Nosara precisely because it isn't anywhere else. It also sits in the Nicoya Peninsula Blue Zone, one of five places on earth where people live measurably longer.

The market reflects the scarcity: some of the strongest appreciation on the coast, with tight supply and a wealthier, longer-hold buyer. It's a different economics than Tamarindo — I've compared the two directly in Tamarindo vs. Nosara if you're weighing them.
