Guanacaste's Communities & Developments.

The gated communities and resort developments where people actually buy — what each offers, who it fits, and the homes for sale in each right now.

Town pages tell you where to live; these pages tell you what you are actually buying into. Every community below is one I show and sell in. HOA fees, rental rules and construction guidelines change and are rarely published, so where a number matters I confirm the current figure directly rather than quoting a stale one.

Avellanas
Hacienda Pinilla
4,500 gated acres between Tamarindo and Avellanas — golf, beach club, three miles of coastline, and the deepest resale market of any resort community on this coast.
Brasilito
Reserva Conchal
Guanacaste's most complete resort community — Robert Trent Jones II golf, the Westin and W hotels, and a beach club on Playa Conchal itself.
Tamarindo
Senderos
Tamarindo's largest luxury gated community — 110 hillside acres above town with a private beach club at the estuary and 'Natural Modern' architecture.
Tamarindo
Tamarindo Park
The newest gated community in Tamarindo — a planned 220-home village in the reforested hills between Tamarindo and Langosta, designed by Richard Müller.
Playa Grande
Las Ventanas de Playa Grande
A 380-acre, 73-property community on the ridge between Playa Grande and Playa Conchal — the lowest-density gated community on this coast.
Brasilito
Mar Vista
750 acres between Brasilito and Flamingo — 5,000 m² lots, 30 km of paved roads, a third of the land preserved, and La Paz school at the entrance.
Brasilito
Catalina Cove
An established 40-hectare community in Brasilito — around 100 oversized lots, mature trees, full-time neighbors, and some of the most accessible entry prices behind this coastline.
Est. 2005
Las Catalinas
Las Catalinas
The car-free New Urbanist beach town on Playa Danta — walkable streets, 1,000 acres of tropical forest trails, and architecture found nowhere else in Central America.
